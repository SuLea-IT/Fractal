26 INTRCODUCTION
27 Wheat (Triticum aestivum L.) is one of the three major global staple crops, requires
C
28 genetic improvement and precision agronomy to enhance its yield for meeting
2A9 worldwide food demands1. Optimizing spike architecture (particularly regulating
30 spikelet fertility and grain arrangement) represents an effective strategy for yield
31 improvement. The wheat spike is a compound spike inflorescence with alternately
32 arranged spikelets along the rachis2. In hexaploidy wheat, a single spike typically
33 bears 15-30 spikelets, each capable of developing 3-5 fertile florets2,3. Florets consist
34 of structures including awns, lemma, palea, anthers, and carpels2, with their
35 developmental progression directly determining spike morphology and grain
36 numbers 4,5.
37 Recent advances in multi-omics integration have accelerated the dissection of spike
